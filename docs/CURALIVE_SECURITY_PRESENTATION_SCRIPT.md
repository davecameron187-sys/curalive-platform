## SECTION 2: THE SOLUTION (5 minutes)

### Slide 3: How CuraLive Protects Your Events
**Speaker Notes:**

"CuraLive uses **three layers of protection** to keep your events safe and compliant:

**Layer 1: Real-Time Detection**

CuraLive continuously monitors your event for compliance violations. It's like having 100 compliance officers listening at the same time, never getting tired, never missing anything.

What does it detect?
- Insider information being disclosed
- Confidential business details being shared
- Profanity or inappropriate language
- Regulatory violations specific to your industry

The moment a violation is detected, your operator is alerted. They see exactly what was said, who said it, and when. They can then take action immediately.

**Real-world example:** During an earnings call, a CFO accidentally mentions that the company is acquiring a competitor. CuraLive detects this in real-time. The operator is alerted within seconds. The speaker can be notified, or if necessary, muted to prevent further disclosure.

**Layer 2: Automated Response**

For serious violations, CuraLive can automatically take action. We call this **Phase 2 Auto-Muting**.

When a speaker violates compliance rules repeatedly, CuraLive can automatically mute them. We have two types of muting:

1. **Soft mute** — The speaker is muted for 30 seconds. This gives them a chance to realize they made a mistake and correct themselves. After 30 seconds, they're automatically unmuted.

2. **Hard mute** — The speaker is muted permanently. This is used for serious violations or repeat offenders.

Your operator always has the final say. If a soft mute was triggered by accident, they can manually unmute the speaker. If a hard mute was too harsh, they can override it.

**Real-world example:** A speaker mentions insider information twice during an earnings call. After the second violation, CuraLive automatically applies a soft mute. The speaker realizes they made a mistake, takes a breath, and when they're unmuted 30 seconds later, they're more careful with their words.

**Layer 3: Complete Audit Trail**

Everything is recorded in an **immutable audit trail**. This means:
- Every violation is logged with a timestamp
- Every action taken (alert, mute, unmute) is recorded
- Who took the action and when is documented
- The record can't be altered or deleted

This audit trail is invaluable for compliance. If a regulator asks, "Did you detect that violation? What did you do about it?" You can show them the exact record.

**Real-world example:** The SEC investigates a potential insider trading violation during one of your earnings calls. You can pull up the CuraLive audit trail and show them: violation detected at 2:47 PM, operator alerted at 2:47:03 PM, speaker muted at 2:47:15 PM. Complete transparency."

## SECTION 3: SECURITY FOUNDATION (4 minutes)

### Slide 4: How We Protect Your Data
**Speaker Notes:**

"Now, all of this detection and muting doesn't matter if your data isn't secure. So let me walk you through how CuraLive protects your information.

**Encryption: Your Data is Locked Down**

Think of encryption like a secure vault. All your data—transcripts, recordings, compliance alerts—is locked in encrypted vaults. Even if someone tries to steal it, they can't read it.

Here's how it works:
- When your operator uploads a recording, it's encrypted before it leaves their computer
- It stays encrypted while it travels to our servers
- It stays encrypted while it's stored in our database
- It stays encrypted when it's downloaded

The encryption keys are managed by industry-leading security providers. Even our own engineers can't decrypt your data without the proper authorization.

**Access Control: Only the Right People See Your Data**

We use a strict 'need-to-know' principle. Just like a bank teller can only access customer accounts they're authorized to manage, CuraLive users can only access the data they're supposed to see.

Let me give you some examples:
- An operator at Company A cannot see any data from Company B's events, even if they try to access it directly
- A compliance officer can see violations and audit trails, but not raw audio or video
- A presenter can see their own teleprompter data, but not attendee information
- An attendee can see publicly available information, but not internal discussions

This is enforced at the database level. There's no way around it.

**Authentication: Proving You Are Who You Say You Are**

Before anyone can access CuraLive, they must prove their identity. We use industry-standard authentication methods:
- Single sign-on (SSO) integration with your company's identity system
- Secure session management with automatic timeout
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) available for extra security
- Automatic logout after inactivity

**Real-world example:** Your team logs in with their company email and password. If they leave their computer unattended, CuraLive automatically logs them out after 30 minutes. If someone tries to access their account from a suspicious location, they're asked to verify their identity with a second factor (like a code from their phone)."

## PRESENTER TIPS

### Delivery Guidelines

**Tone:** Professional, confident, transparent
- Speak with authority (you know this material)
- Be honest about limitations (no system is 100% secure)
- Use real-world examples to make concepts relatable

**Pacing:** 30 minutes for presentation, 10 minutes for Q&A
- Don't rush through the security foundation section (it's the most important)
- Spend extra time on compliance features (this is what customers care about)
- Be ready for technical questions (have the tech stack brief available)

**Engagement:** Keep the audience engaged
- Use real-world examples throughout
- Ask rhetorical questions ("What's the biggest risk when you host a live event?")
- Pause for questions at the end of each section if time allows
- Make eye contact with the audience

**Visual Aids:** If using slides
- Keep slides simple (one main idea per slide)
- Use visuals to illustrate concepts (encryption vault, access control matrix)
- Don't read directly from slides (use them as a guide)
- Include company logos (AWS, Stripe, Twilio, etc.) when discussing partners

### Audience-Specific Adaptations

**For C-Suite / Board Members:**
- Focus on risk mitigation and competitive advantage
- Emphasize SOC 2 certification and regulatory compliance
- Highlight the business impact (avoiding fines, protecting reputation)
- Keep technical details to a minimum

**For Compliance Officers:**
- Focus on violation detection and audit trails
- Dive deep into industry-specific regulations (SEC, HIPAA, etc.)
- Discuss compliance reports and regulatory submissions
- Provide detailed examples of how compliance features work

**For Security Teams:**
- Focus on technical architecture and security controls
- Discuss encryption, access controls, and authentication
- Explain incident response procedures and SLAs
- Provide information about third-party security assessments

**For Legal Teams:**
- Focus on data privacy and user rights
- Discuss Data Processing Agreements and litigation holds
- Explain data retention and deletion policies
- Discuss regulatory compliance and eDiscovery support
