Product 2: Investor Communication Intelligence Platform
This product would sell communication intelligence data to the market.
Think of it as:
“The Bloomberg-style intelligence layer for earnings call communication.”
Instead of only analyzing a company’s own events, the platform analyzes communication patterns across thousands of companies.
________________________________________
What This Platform Would Provide
The system aggregates signals from many investor events.
Example signals:
•	investor sentiment during calls
•	difficult investor questions
•	executive response patterns
•	topic discussion trends
•	engagement levels
This creates a market-level communication dataset.
________________________________________
Example Intelligence Products
Investor Concern Intelligence
Tracks the topics investors are asking about globally.
Example insight:
Top investor concerns this quarter

1. AI infrastructure spending
2. Margin pressure
3. Supply chain resilience
Investors and strategists love this type of insight.
________________________________________
Executive Communication Benchmarking
Ranks how executives handle investor questions.
Example output:
Executive Communication Score

Industry Average: 72
Company CEO: 84
Peer Benchmark: 76
Boards and investors care about executive communication quality.
________________________________________
Market Reaction Correlation
Links communication patterns to market outcomes.
Example insight:
Earnings calls where revenue guidance was avoided
→ 58% probability of negative stock reaction
This connects communication behavior with financial performance.
________________________________________
The Target Customers
This second platform can serve a completely different group of customers.
Potential users include:
•	hedge funds
•	asset managers
•	investment banks
•	equity analysts
•	financial research firms
These groups already pay heavily for financial intelligence tools.
________________________________________
Why This Product Could Become More Valuable
Corporate SaaS typically scales slowly because each company must be sold individually.
Data platforms scale differently.
Once CuraLive builds a large dataset of investor communications, the intelligence products can be sold to thousands of financial professionals simultaneously.
This is how large financial data platforms grow.
Examples include companies like
Bloomberg LP, FactSet, and S&P Global.
These companies became extremely valuable because they own financial datasets that others depend on.
